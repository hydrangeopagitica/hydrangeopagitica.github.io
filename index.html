Areopagitica - from Latin Areopagus, < Greek Ἄρειος πάγος (Áreios págos, “the hill of Ares”), the site of the highest court in classical Athens. “Areopagitica” was the title of a 1644 anti-censorship tract written by John Milton, though I initially assumed the word referred to a kind of peach-shaped, steam-powered dirigible.

Hydriotaphia - from Greek ὑδρία (hydríā, “water jar”) + τάφος (táphos, “tomb”). Hydriotaphia, or more properly Hydriotaphia, Urne-Buriall, or, A Discourse of the Sepulchrall Urnes lately found in Norfolk is a 1658 book by Thomas Browne on the topic of funerary rites, though I initially assumed the word referred to an obscure species of the genus Hydrangea.

Hydrangeopagitica - from English Hydriotaphia and English Areopagitica. “Hydrangeopagitica” refers to the leisurely and amateur pursuit of misinterpretation, though I initially assumed it was the title of this blog.
